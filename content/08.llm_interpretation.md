## Supplementary Section SS1: LLM-assisted biological interpretation of KF-CHD and KF-NBL pathway results

### Overview

The following interpretations were generated by providing the `pathway_results_llm.md` and `pathway_results_summary.tsv` output files from each cohort to a general-purpose large language model (GPT-4o) in a fresh session with no prior context. The model was given the scored pathway table and seed gene list as structured input and asked to interpret the biological meaning of the results, followed by a direct request to mine the TSV. No method-specific guidance was provided beyond the session framing embedded in the output file. These outputs are illustrative of the exploratory biological interpretation use case for `pathway_results_llm.md` and are not part of the quantitative evaluation of BIFO.

---

### SS1.1 KF-CHD: congenital heart disease cohort (697 probands, 1,287 seed genes)

#### Summary interpretation

The clearest biological signal in this KF-CHD analysis is a cilia and cell-architecture program, with secondary themes in membrane transport, inflammation, metabolism, and developmental remodeling. The cohort has 697 probands, 1,287 seed genes, and 265 significant pathways at q < 0.05. The strongest interpretable hit is WP_CILIOPATHIES at rank 43 with null_z = 41.19, and the significant set also includes regulation of cell shape, anchoring of the basal body to the plasma membrane, multiple transport pathways, apoptosis, inflammatory response, and myogenesis.

This does not look like one narrow signaling lesion. It looks more like a developmental systems phenotype centered on cellular organization. For congenital heart disease, that is plausible: primary cilia are important for left-right patterning, morphogen sensing, tissue polarity, and coordinated morphogenesis during cardiogenesis.

The seed list supports this interpretation directly. Even in the excerpted genes, there are multiple classic cilia or centrosome-associated genes including AHI1, ALMS1, ARL13B, ARMC9, B9D1, BBS10, BBS12, BBS4, BBS9, CC2D2A, CEP104, CEP152, CEP250, CEP290, and CEP41 — exactly the seed composition that can drive a biologically meaningful ciliopathy signal in CHD, particularly for defects tied to laterality, outflow tract development, or broader syndromic developmental phenotypes.

#### Biological modules (from TSV mining)

**Module 1: Cilia and developmental cell architecture (primary)**

Core hits: WP_CILIOPATHIES (rank 43, null_z = 41.19, q = 0.0081), REACTOME_CILIUM_ASSEMBLY (rank 163), WP_GENES_RELATED_TO_PRIMARY_CILIUM_DEVELOPMENT_BASED_ON_CRISPR (rank 171), regulation of cell shape (rank 51), REACTOME_ANCHORING_OF_THE_BASAL_BODY_TO_THE_PLASMA_MEMBRANE (rank 95).

Multiple independent pathway definitions converge on the same biology — this is a true module. Importantly, the pre-specified cilia reference pathways are enriched while several canonical Hedgehog pathway entries show negative null_z values and q = 1.0, suggesting the CHD signal is more strongly centered on ciliary structure and basal body function than on broad Hedgehog transcriptional programs. A precise biological framing is: ciliary structure and trafficking with selective overlap to Hedgehog-related biology, rather than global enrichment of all Hedgehog pathway definitions.

**Module 2: Membrane transport and trafficking (strong secondary)**

Disorders of transmembrane transporters, SLC-mediated transport, ion/cation/anion transport, receptor-mediated endocytosis, and scavenger receptor uptake form a coherent module. In CHD this may reflect ion flux relevant to cardiomyocyte function, membrane trafficking relevant to morphogenesis, and transporter biology shared with ciliary compartments and polarized epithelia.

**Module 3: Inflammatory and innate immune signaling (secondary)**

Inflammatory response, IL4/IL13, IL18, TLR cascades, antigen processing, and complement are present but rank below the cilia-cell-shape module in mechanistic priority. In a germline CHD setting, these more likely reflect pleiotropic developmental genes, extracellular remodeling, or syndromic biology.

**Module 4: Metabolism (supporting layer)**

Adipogenesis, lipid metabolism, fatty acid metabolism, carbohydrate metabolism, TCA cycle, and broader metabolic terms form a distributed but coherent module, likely reflecting a broad developmental and organelle-linked program rather than primary metabolic disease.

**Module 5: Cell cycle and DNA maintenance (supporting)**

G2M checkpoint, DNA damage response, DNA replication, Fanconi pathway, and DNA repair appear as a supporting module, likely reflecting proliferative developmental processes or syndromic developmental gene overlap.

**Module 6: Muscle and cardiac development (contextual)**

Myogenesis, striated muscle contraction, Notch signaling, and RAAS-related signaling are present, consistent with heart-development biology.

#### Cohort interpretation

The KF-CHD germline variant cohort is most consistent with a developmental systems architecture centered on primary cilium biology, centrosome/basal body function, regulation of cell shape, and membrane transport. Inflammation, metabolism, and cell-cycle pathways are present but secondary to that structural-developmental core.

**One-sentence biological summary:** This KF-CHD germline analysis is most consistent with a developmental architecture signal dominated by ciliary biology, basal body and centrosome function, membrane transport, and regulation of cell shape, converging on altered morphogen sensing, polarity, and tissue patterning during heart development.

### SS1.2 KF-NBL: neuroblastoma cohort (460 probands, 1,406 seed genes)

#### Summary interpretation

The neuroblastoma germline signal resolves into a coherent biology centered on membrane transport and ionic homeostasis, cilium and sensory-organelle biology, cell shape and developmental architecture, DNA repair, and broad metabolic liability. The cohort has 460 probands, 1,406 seed genes, 2,196 pathways scored, and 296 significant pathways at q < 0.05. Top-ranked pathways include Ionophore activity (rank 1), Transmembrane Transport (rank 2), WP_CILIOPATHIES (rank 3, null_z = 18.4, q = 0.014), Regulation of Cell Shape, and DNA Repair (rank 5).

The seed list supports this directly: recognizable neuroblastoma and developmental genes including ALK, ATRX, BARD1, BCOR, BRCA2, and BLM appear alongside multiple cilia genes (AHI1, ALMS1, ARL13B, B9D1, BBS10, BBS4, BBS9) and transporters (ATP1A3, ATP7B, ABCA/ABCB family, calcium-channel genes).

#### Biological modules (from TSV mining)

**Module 1: Cilium and developmental cell architecture (primary, high specificity)**

Core hits: WP_CILIOPATHIES (rank 3), REACTOME_CILIUM_ASSEMBLY, WP_GENES_RELATED_TO_PRIMARY_CILIUM_DEVELOPMENT, WP_JOUBERT_SYNDROME, regulation of cell shape, organelle biogenesis and maintenance, histogenic process.

Multiple independent pathway definitions converge on the same biology. In neuroblastoma, this maps to neural crest-derived cell morphogenesis, polarity, migration, and signaling competence during development. Sensory-pathway terms (vision, rhodopsin, phototransduction, cochlear processing, smell perception) are best interpreted as proxies for ciliary and ion-channel biology rather than literal tissue-specific signals.

**Module 2: Membrane transport and ion handling (very strong, dominant by count)**

Top hits: Ionophore activity (rank 1), Transmembrane Transport (rank 2), SLC transporter disorders, homeostasis, synaptic transmission. Approximately 721 significant pathways map to this theme — a global property of the gene set, not a one-off enrichment.

In neuroblastoma, this maps to neuronal differentiation and signaling competence: sympathoadrenal lineage cells depend on transporters, channels, vesicular handling, and membrane potential-related programs. This likely reflects inherited perturbation of how precursor cells sense extracellular cues, maintain ionic balance, and route developmental signals.

**Module 3: DNA repair and genome maintenance (moderate, high confidence)**

Core hits: DNA Repair (rank 5), recombinational repair, mismatch repair, nucleotide excision repair, Reactome DNA Repair. Approximately 64 significant pathways, null_z values ~7–11.

This is a clean, specific module not prominent in the CHD cohort. It provides an oncogenic backbone: increased mutational susceptibility and impaired replication stress response. Known neuroblastoma predisposition genes (ATM, ATR, ATRX, BARD1, BLM, BRCA2, BRIP1, APTX) directly support this axis.

**Module 4: Metabolism (broad, distributed)**

Amino acid, carbohydrate, nucleotide, xenobiotic, and mitochondrial tRNA aminoacylation pathways (~123 significant pathways). Likely reflects organelle function and coupling to transport processes rather than a primary disease driver.

**Module 5: Developmental and morphogenesis programs (supporting)**

Skeletal development, organogenesis, myogenesis, and histogenic processes reinforce the cilium signal as shared developmental architecture, consistent with a tumor of disrupted developmental origin.

**Module 6: ECM, coagulation, complement (contextual)**

Blood coagulation, complement activation, ECM organization, heparin binding. Likely reflecting extracellular signaling, neural crest migration, and adhesion programs.

#### Cohort interpretation

The germline neuroblastoma cohort reflects inherited variation converging on neurodevelopmental cell architecture, ciliary and trafficking functions, genome maintenance, and membrane-metabolic homeostasis. The DNA repair module is the most distinctive feature relative to the CHD cohort.

**One-sentence biological summary:** The KF-NBL germline burden is most consistent with inherited vulnerability in neurodevelopmental cell-state control, especially cilium-dependent signaling, membrane transport, and morphogenetic architecture, layered with a meaningful DNA-repair component and a broad metabolic support program.
